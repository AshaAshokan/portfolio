Overview

Notification banners can be used to display various types of messages, such as alerts, warnings, updates, or confirmations. They usually include a short text message, sometimes along with an icon or a call-to-action button, and can be dismissed by the user or automatically disappear after a certain period of time. User interface elements that display short messages to users, typically at the top of the screen.
Specific use case: Meeting Reminders, Incoming Call Alerts, Joining a Call, Dismissal and Interaction.

Current notification component

I noticed that the current notification component has usability issues: Limited visibility, User confusion, Space constraints, Technical limitations. Considering these potential problems, I followed design patterns and guidelines for notifications to ensure clarity, consistency, and optimal user experience.  The components needed to be designed in a way that was consistent with our brand guidelines and adaptable to different user interfaces.

Requirements

In this case i gathered information based on the following aspects :

Functional behaviours
  • Banners are reserved for actionable information. Tapping on a banner or its call-to-actions should always trigger an event.
  • These are considered more disruptive than toasts and should be used sparingly for only urgent information.
  • These can self dismiss if no longer relevant (e.g. incoming call never answered) but expect user interaction.
  • Banners support at most two actions — a primary and secondary. The secondary action should be a dismiss action, as a discoverable and consistent way to dismiss the banner. The swipe right gesture is supplementary, but shouldn’t be the only mechanism to dismiss.

Foundations
  • Uniqueness: Is not redundant with other Product System components.
  • Color: Uses BDG semantic colors, following usage guidelines.
  • Color Contrast: Meets AA color contrast ratio
  • Typography: Uses BDG Type Ramp
Assets
  • Icons: Uses instances for icons from the BDG System Icon Library.
  • Illustrations: Follows BDG illustration guidelines.
  • Motion: Follows BDG motion guidelines.
Layout
  • Touch Targets: Touch targets are at least 44x44dp, with the BDG standard of 24dp of space.Text links should have a min surface area of 1936dp.
  • Spacing: Spacing adheres to multiples of an 8dp, or 4dp for under 24dp.
  • Localization & Font Scaling: Accommodates for all languages and type sizes by defining component behaviour for longer, shorter, or larger text.
  • Content: Content adheres to the Content Style Guide.
States, Variants & Modes
  • States: Pressed, disabled, loading and error states are included if applicable.
  • Dark Mode: Includes dark mode variants
  • Accessibility Labels: Includes accessiblity labels when applicable.
  • Sounds and Screen reader.

Best Practice

✅ Do

Do use banners for urgent prompts for making a choice or interaction, that merits disrupting a person’s current context.

❌ Don’t

Do not use banners for promotional purposes.

✅ Do

Keep content brief and glanceable.

❌ Don’t

Don’t bury critical information that can’t be gleaned quickly, as the banner doesn’t appear for long.

✅ Do

Place the primary action to the left, as it allows the dismissive secondary action to live on the edges of the container.

❌ Don’t

Don’t place the primary action on the right, as it leads to a secondary dismissive action to live in the middle of the banner.
