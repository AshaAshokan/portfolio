Identifying pain points

After interviewing with current users and people who had never seen the product before, it became clear that there were several issues with the current user experience which manufactured hidden features and clutter in the meeting window.

Primary and secondary research was conducted in order to get familiar with different video conferencing platforms, user opinions, their experience, wishes and needs. Based on the current users and new users feedback and suggestions we figured few pain points that needed to cover.

------Research Insights

  • Banners are reserved for actionable information. Tapping on a banner or its call-to-actions should always trigger an event.
  • These are considered more disruptive than toasts and should be used sparingly for only urgent information.
  • These can self dismiss if no longer relevant (e.g. incoming call never answered) but expect user interaction.
  • Banners support at most two actions — a primary and secondary. The secondary action should be a dismiss action, as a discoverable and consistent way to dismiss the banner. The swipe right gesture is supplementary, but shouldn’t be the only mechanism to dismiss.

we came to the conclusion to revamp the entire PP module without affecting the existing long time users who used to be with the current UX/UI since we are not doing something from scratch.

Final Designs

Usability testing:

After the visual design was complete I tested the prototype with a few users to see how user-friendly the application is. The test was in Zoho meeting video calls where the participants were given the following tasks while I observed how they navigated through the application.

  • Create instant meeting
  • Start meeting
  • Invite participants/share meeting link
  • On/off audio and video
  • Taking notes
  • Chat
  • Share screen
  • Record meeting
  • End meeting

The participants were able to navigate through the app easily. They appreciated the interface and gave the following inputs.

Suggestions to make the experience better

  • Found it is difficult to locate the request control button while the screen is being shared.
  • On the join meeting page not sure about the name was editable
  • The dark theme gives a dull experience for a few (was in the roadmap)
  • There is a chance of clicking the exit button accidentally

We took serious about the feedback and covered and tested it again with the users.

Conclusion:

Apart from revamping the meeting app other possibilities include focusing on enhancing, meeting toolbar minimal for anything you need during a meeting. On the whole, I have learnt a lot more about user research, to use and create symbols/components, create own icons and illustrations
